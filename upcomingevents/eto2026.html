ETO Eastern 2026

Where emerging technology meets people, projects, and possibility.

Join a two-day conference in Hong Kong where students, researchers, builders, educators, and organisations come together around Blockchain, AI, and Quantum Computing to showcase projects, exchange ideas, and discover what comes next.

  • 📅 10–11 October 2026

Why ETO Eastern

A platform, not just a contest

ETO Eastern is not just a competition, and it is not just a conference. It is a public platform where projects are presented, ideas are challenged, and students, judges, universities, companies, and partners meet in one shared space.

Some people join to compete. Others join to observe, judge, speak, exhibit, recruit, or build partnerships. That is why ETO Eastern is designed around two tracks: a judged Competition Track and a wider Community Track.

What to expect

Five experiences across two days

Project Expo

Teams present posters, prototypes, and ideas in a live showcase format.

Pitch Stage

Shortlisted teams move into a more visible, higher-stakes presentation format.

Socratic Circle

A reflective discussion space for bigger questions about technology and society.

AGI Expo

Universities, companies, research bodies, and public organisations share opportunities and meet participants.

Awards & Closing

The event ends with public recognition for standout work and a formal closing ceremony.

What kind of projects?

Three domains. One competition.

Blockchain & Trust Technologies
BCTT

Design and present a real-world blockchain application addressing a societal or commercial challenge. Strong projects should show technical soundness, innovation, feasibility, and clear communication.

Team size: 2–5 members
Data Science & AI
DSAI

Develop a data-driven research project or AI application that addresses a real-world problem. Strong projects should show analytical rigour, methodological clarity, and actionable insight.

Team size: 2–5 members
Quantum Computing & Information Theory
QCIT

Investigate a quantum computing algorithm, application, or conceptual framework. Strong projects should show understanding of quantum principles and explain their real-world or future relevance.

Team size: 2–5 members

How judging works

Four shared criteria

Projects are assessed across four shared criteria, with expectations adjusted for Schoolers and Students streams.

30%

Technical Rigour

Depth of research, methodological soundness, and technical correctness

25%

Innovation

Originality, creativity, and novel application

25%

Real-World Impact

Practical relevance and societal or commercial value

20%

Presentation

Clarity, structure, confidence, and Q&A quality

What observers can do

Experience the conference

Observers are non-competing attendees interested in emerging technologies. They can attend open sessions, explore the expo, watch public pitches, and take part in the broader conference atmosphere, but they do not judge projects or participate in formal contestant Q&A.

Community theme

Socratic Circle 2026

One of the key non-competitive parts of ETO Eastern is the Socratic Circle, a moderated discussion format designed to bring reflection and dialogue into the event, not just pitching and showcasing.

2026 theme

Education for the next decade to come

This theme invites participants to explore how emerging technologies may reshape learning, access, institutions, and opportunity in the years ahead.

Why join

More than a contest

The Community Track exists because ETO Eastern is designed as a gathering and platform, not only a contest. It gives organisations access to promising projects and people, and it gives the public a way to experience the event without having to compete.
