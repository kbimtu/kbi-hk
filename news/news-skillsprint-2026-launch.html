Launching Emerging-Technology
SkillSprint (ETS) 2026

Intensive workshops in blockchain, data, and AI — built for serious beginners.

KBI HK Programmes Team
KBI Hong Kong
8 May 2026
4 min read

After a successful 2025 edition at City University of Hong Kong, KBI Hong Kong is launching Emerging-Technology SkillSprint (ETS) 2026 — the 2026 edition of our SkillSprint workshop series covering blockchain, trust technologies, data science, and AI for students who want a serious, hands-on introduction to the field.

SkillSprint is not an expo or a showcase. It is a small, focused environment where participants work directly with concepts, tools, and problems that real teams face.

What SkillSprint is

The 2026 edition — ETS — is designed for:

  • Students interested in emerging technologies who want to move beyond surface-level familiarity.
  • Participants preparing for competitions (IBCOL, IDSOL, IQCOL, ETO) who need stronger foundations.
  • Members curious about how blockchain, data, and AI actually fit together in real systems.

Each SkillSprint edition is short and intense, technical but guided, and applied — exercises are grounded in realistic scenarios, not isolated toy problems. The 2025 series ran as FinTech SkillSprint at CityU; 2026 broadens the lens to emerging technologies across the same concentrated format.

What's new in 2026

The 2026 edition builds on community feedback from 2025:

  • Clearer tracks for different starting points (for example, first-time learners vs competition-ready).
  • More structured lab components and fewer one-off demos.
  • Integrated coverage of AI tools, including how to use them responsibly in analysis, prototyping, and documentation.

How it connects to other programmes

SkillSprint is part of the broader KBI HK pathway. It helps newcomers build enough foundation to participate more confidently in olympiads and projects; gives experienced participants a way to refresh or deepen understanding; and can be a stepping stone towards more advanced training and, eventually, ISFRETIC-aligned certification pathways.

Who should consider joining

ETS is a good fit if you are a secondary or university student with genuine interest in emerging technologies; have participated in KBI programmes and want more structured learning; or are new to KBI but looking for a serious first engagement, not simply an info session.

Next steps

Full dates, host venue, and registration details will be announced on the Events page and through KBI HK's mailing list. Members will receive early access to registration.
