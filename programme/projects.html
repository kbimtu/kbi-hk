Project Support

Helping promising ideas move beyond the competition stage.

KBI supports student teams in taking strong ideas further — beyond pitches, beyond judging rounds, and into deeper development. Through mentorship, project guidance, and connections with academic and industry partners, we help selected teams explore what it takes to turn an early concept into something more substantial.

What It Is

Beyond competition

Competitions are often where ideas begin. Project support is what comes next.

Some teams leave a competition with more than a presentation. They leave with a concept that deserves another round of thinking, testing, or building. KBI’s Project Support pathway exists to help those ideas continue — with clearer direction, stronger feedback, and, where appropriate, access to a broader ecosystem of collaborators and partners.

What Support Can Look Like

How KBI supports projects

Support does not always look the same. Different ideas need different kinds of help, and the level of support may vary depending on the project’s maturity, team readiness, and partner interest.

  • Mentorship from researchers, practitioners, or senior community members.
  • Feedback on scope, feasibility, and next-step development.
  • Connections to academic or industry contexts where a concept may be tested further.
  • Support in shaping a proof of concept, prototype, dataset, paper, or other concrete output.
  • Referral to relevant KBI opportunities, including bursaries or future project pathways.
Who It Is For

Who this is for

Project Support is intended for students and teams whose work shows unusual promise and who are ready to keep developing it after an event, challenge, or internal KBI programme.

Not every competition idea becomes a supported project, and not every supported project follows the same path. The point is not to promise scale too early, but to give good ideas the environment they need to be examined seriously.

What Outcomes May Emerge

What it can lead to

In some cases, supported ideas remain exploratory. In others, they may develop into proof-of-concepts, research collaborations, open tools, public-facing demonstrations, or projects adopted in some form by academic or industry partners.

We do not treat every project as a startup, and we do not force every idea into the same model. What matters is whether the work is worth continuing — and whether the team is ready to grow with it.

Closing

From idea to next step

Project Support is one of the ways KBI helps student work continue after the applause ends. If your team has developed an idea with genuine potential, we want there to be a pathway forward.

More details on formats, eligibility, and current opportunities will be shared as this pathway develops.
