About KBI Hong Kong

Our History

Our history begins with a simple premise: if you treat students like serious technologists, they will rise to that standard.

The Story

Where We Started

KBI Hong Kong did not begin with a grant, an institutional mandate, or a room full of advisors. It began with a small group of people who believed Hong Kong’s students deserved serious emerging-technology education — and decided to build it themselves.

Nine years later, that decision has impacted thousands of lives . This is how it happened.

2017–2019

Early Years

KBI Hong Kong was established in 2017 and marking the inaugural International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL). Convened by Dr. Lawrence Ma, the finals were co-hosted with the University of Waterloo and City University of Hong Kong. This period laid the groundwork for a more structured and ambitious emerging technology programme ecosystem.

2020–2022

Transition and Expansion

Between 2020 and 2022, KBI Hong Kong underwent significant transformation. The COVID-19 pandemic necessitated a shift from in-person programming to online and hybrid formats.

In 2020, the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) was introduced, debuting in a hybrid format that combined virtual sessions with in-person components at City University of Hong Kong alongside IBCOL. During this time, KBI Hong Kong not only adapted to disruption but also expanded its academic scope, offering students access to a broader range of emerging technology disciplines.

2023–2024

Return to In-Person Global Engagement

With the easing of pandemic restrictions, in-person events resumed. The Emerging Technologies Olympiad (ETO), comprising IBCOL and IDSOL, was hosted at Hogeschool Inholland in Amsterdam.

In December 2024, the Canadian International School of Hong Kong celebrated notable student achievements at the Amsterdam finals, including a gold medal in IDSOL and a top-three placement in IBCOL.

2025–Present

Structuring for the Next Decade

This period marks a new phase of strategic development and regional reintegration for KBI Hong Kong, alongside the return of ETO to Asia.

In 2025, the ETO international finals were hosted at Diocesan Girls’ School and Swire Blueprint in Hong Kong, complemented by cross-border side events in Shenzhen to strengthen regional engagement.

In 2026, the Emerging Technologies Olympiad – Eastern Conference is scheduled to take place at the Canadian International School of Hong Kong, alongside the launch of the inaugural International Quantum Computing Olympiad.

Founding Philosophy

Looking Ahead

"Hong Kong's students are capable of operating at the international frontier of emerging technology — they just need programmes serious enough to take them there."

This was the founding premise in 2016, and it remains the organising principle of everything KBI Hong Kong builds. Nine years of sustained operation have validated that premise — but the work is far from finished.
